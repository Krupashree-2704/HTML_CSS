Millet Information

Sorghum Millet (Jowar)

1. Jowar is globally known as the “new quinoa” for its gluten-free and whole-grain goodness.

2. It goes by the name of Sorghum in English.

3. In India, jowar is a staple food item.

Ingridients :

Jowar Idli

Jowar Chakli

Jowar Roti

Pearl Millet (Bajra)

1. Bajra is a traditional Hindi name for the Pennisetum glaucum crop — also known as pearl millet.

2. It’s likewise known as dukn, cumbu, gero, sanio, kambu, babala, or bulrush millet (1).

3. The grain is primarily grown in Africa and India, where it’s a major source of nutrition.

4. However, it’s also grown and consumed in many other places around the world.

5. Bajra refers to the edible seeds of pearl millet plants.

6. They grow in various shades of white, yellow, gray, brown, and bluish-purple.

7. The seeds are typically cooked as a cereal grain or sometimes finely ground and used as a flour.

Ingridients :

Bajra Roti

Bajra Kichdi

Bajra Methi

Finger Millet (Ragi)

1. Finger millet originated in Africa and has been cultivated for many thousands of years in Uganda and Ethiopia.

2. In India, the crop was probably introduced 4000 years ago

3. It has been found in archeological excavations in the Harappan Civilization.

Ingridients :

Ragi Chapati

Ragi Idli

Ragi Pancake

Little Millet (Moraiyo)

1. Moraiyo is also known as Sava in Marathi, Gajro in Gujarati, Saame in Kannada, Samai in Tamil, and Samalu in Telugu.

2. It is a traditional crop which is grown across India and is suitable for all age groups.

3. It is a relative of Proso Millet but the seeds of little millets are smaller when compared to the Proso Millet.

4. It is highly nutritious and we can consume it as rice.

5. All the dishes that require staple rice can be prepared using Little Millet as it has the small seed and can be cooked faster than other similar grains.

Ingridients :

Little Millet Poha

Little Millet Poori

Little Millet Rice
